NEVI Formula Program — Maintenance Mandate
Active — TX Q2/Q3 2026
Every NEVI-funded station must maintain >97% average annual uptime per port on a 12-month rolling basis. NEVI funds directly cover O&M costs for up to 5 years after station commissioning, including hardware/software maintenance, repair costs, and service agreements. All technicians must hold EVITP certification or equivalent. Station operators can delegate maintenance to subcontractors. Approximately 4,000 NEVI-funded DCFC ports awarded nationally across ~990 sites — at $600–800+/port annually, this is a substantial recurring revenue market.

The EV Charger Reliability and Accessibility Accelerator (EVC-RAA) specifically funds repair or replacement of broken existing chargers. Washington State ($9.5M) and Oregon ($10M) programs open to private entities.

CMS Emergency Preparedness Rule — Permanent Mandate
Permanent — No Grant Dependency
17+ provider/supplier types — hospitals, nursing homes, psychiatric hospitals, ESRD facilities, ASCs — must maintain emergency power systems as condition of Medicare/Medicaid participation. NFPA 99 and NFPA 110 require monthly testing, fuel management, and preventive maintenance. Joint Commission mandates 30-minute tests monthly and 4-hour continuous tests every 36 months. This is an unfunded mandate — neither generators nor maintenance are reimbursable by CMS. Creates permanent, mandatory market independent of any grant program.
