Texas Energy Fund (TxEF)
Active
Legislature appropriated $5B for FY2024–2025 and an additional $4B for FY2026–2027. Funds grants and loans for construction, maintenance, and modernization of electric facilities. The Texas Backup Power Package is the angle for Apollo's generator services — funds standalone multi-day backup power for hospitals, nursing homes, and critical facilities. Direct match for Apollo's generator maintenance division.

Strategic Guidance
Highest-ROI Targets for Apollo
  • 1
    Section 30C tax credits on any LNG/CNG/EV fueling infrastructure installed at Apollo terminals — straightforward, available now, expires June 30, 2026. Claim on every qualifying installation immediately.
  • 2
    NEVI subcontracting through L-Charge or other CPOs as Texas releases its Q2/Q3 2026 solicitation. Apollo's installation and field services capability is the differentiator.
  • 3
    USDA REAP for rural terminal capex in Arkansas, Oklahoma, and Tennessee — 50% grant + 75% guaranteed loan structure is highly favorable for capital-intensive terminal builds.
  • 4
    Texas Backup Power Package (TxEF) for Apollo's generator services division — hospitals and critical facilities are the target customers, and the program funds exactly the multi-day standby power Apollo installs and maintains.
  • 5
    TCEQ AFFP next cycle — watch for the next application window. Position Apollo's LNG/CNG fueling infrastructure in the 88-county CTZ now; be ready to apply day one of the next round.
  • 6
    DOE SBIR (longer-term) — worth exploring if Apollo formalizes the AI/automation work (OpenClaw, TMS V2, MoltBot) into a fundable R&D proposal. Heavier lift but $200K–$1.1M available for genuine innovation.
